# SimpliXio Reorganization Plan (2026-04-29)

## 1. Values-alignment audit
- Strong: clear wedge in onboarding + README, ranking remains product-owned, safety defaults exist.
- Drifting: copy still inconsistent across all channels, proof cadence was under-structured, too many tactical ideas competing in public narrative.
- Direction: enforce one wedge and one trust standard across product + growth outputs.

## 2. Traction bottlenecks
- Messaging drift across onboarding/screenshots/automation outputs.
- Proof exists but was not packaged as repeatable public-safe artifacts.
- Acquisition had weak boundary between warm signals and internal artifacts.
- Conversion risk from abstract language in some surfaces.

## 3. Strongest wedge recommendation
- Recommended: **SimpliXio turns scattered thoughts, project noise, and open loops into 3 priorities and one next action.**
- Alternative A: Decide what matters now from your captured signals.
- Alternative B: Reduce open loops into one clear daily move.
- Why recommended: strongest input->output clarity and easiest to validate via screenshots and demo.

## 4. Full messaging rewrite recommendations
- One-liner: SimpliXio turns scattered thoughts, project noise, and open loops into 3 priorities and one next action.
- Subtitle: 3 priorities. Why it matters. Next action.
- Onboarding: Turn scattered thoughts and project noise into 3 priorities and one next action.
- Outreach: I saw your prioritization workflow. SimpliXio reduces that overload into 3 priorities and one next move.
- Screenshot headlines:
  1) Decide what matters now.
  2) Turn noise into 3 priorities.
  3) Know why it matters.
  4) Take the next action.
  5) See what was ignored.

## 5. Product-core reorganization plan
- iPhone default: focus on What matters now + 3 priorities + next action + capture in one tap.
- macOS: keep deeper queues and reviews, but not as default cognitive load.
- Watch: stay glance-first (top priority + next action + capture).
- UI rule: no backlog-heavy default surfaces.

## 6. Trust-layer improvements
- Explicit trust copy in onboarding, settings, README, automation docs.
- Private outreach remains `needs_approval`.
- Newsletter and proof outputs stay draft-first with redaction gates.
- Explainability and human control language stays visible.

## 7. Proof-layer improvements
- Standardize weekly proof pack: release notes + build-in-public summary + feedback prompt.
- Use real artifacts only (Today/Weekly Review/Decision Replay/newsletter status).
- Enforce redaction and no-autopublish defaults.

## 8. Acquisition-layer improvements
- Warm-first signals prioritized.
- Internal artifact mining made opt-in (default off).
- Keep fit scoring -> draft outreach -> quality gate flow.
- Keep private outbound manual approval only.

## 9. Integration priority recommendations
1. GitHub
2. Notion
3. Discord
4. RSS
5. Gmail (careful, later-stage)

## 10. Integrations to avoid or postpone
- LinkedIn scraping/automation.
- Broad cold outbound automation.
- Full inbox replacement behavior.
- Extra channels before Tier 1 loops prove signal.

## 11. Discord build-in-public and release-note plan
- Channels: announcements, release-notes, build-in-public, weekly-review, feedback.
- Cadence: weekly curated draft set, manual approval before posting.
- Content blocks: what changed, why it matters, one CTA.

## 12. App Store improvement plan
- Keep first 3 screenshots output-first.
- Keep trust message visible in first 5 slides.
- Keep subtitle and opening hook stable for measurement windows.

## 13. README/GitHub/OpenClaw credibility improvements
- README now centers wedge, trust, and practical outputs.
- Add links to messaging and values plans.
- Keep release notes framed by outcomes, not feature count.

## 14. What to simplify or remove
- Reduce abstract language and broad “platform” framing.
- De-emphasize secondary queues on iPhone default.
- Remove noisy auto-distribution ideas from near-term scope.

## 15. Phased implementation plan
- Phase 1: wedge/trust copy alignment + proof draft automation + warm-first guardrails.
- Phase 2: GitHub/Notion context enrichment tied to ranking inputs.
- Phase 3: approval workflow polish + channel-level growth scorecards.

## 16. First milestone to execute
- Run strict weekly pipeline.
- Review Discord proof drafts.
- Manually publish only one high-signal release note + one weekly proof update.
- Track reply quality and qualified inbound signals.

## 17. What to intentionally postpone
- Autopublishing to public channels.
- Multi-channel breadth before message/fit confirmation.
- Additional integrations not directly improving wedge outcomes.
